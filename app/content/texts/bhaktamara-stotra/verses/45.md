simple_hi: 'गहरी और ऊँची ध्वनि से जिसने सब दिशाओं को भर दिया है, तीनों लोकों के जीवों को शुभ संगम (समवसरण) तक लाने में दक्ष, सद्-धर्म-राज की जय की घोषणा करता हुआ — आकाश में दुन्दुभि (देव-वाद्य) आपके यश की उद्घोषणा करते हुए बजता है।'

simple_en: 'The drum, dundubhi — its deep and high tones filling every quarter, expert at gathering the beings of the three worlds for the auspicious assembly, proclaiming the victory of the king of true *dharma* — sounds in the sky, announcing Your glory.'

translation_hi: 'जिसकी गम्भीर और तीव्र ध्वनि से सम्पूर्ण दिशा-विभाग पूरित हो गए हैं, जो तीनों लोकों के जीवों को शुभ-संगम (समवसरण) के लिए एकत्र करने में दक्ष है, जो सद्-धर्म-राज की विजय का घोषक है — ऐसा दुन्दुभि आकाश में आपके यश का बखान करता हुआ बजता है।'

translation_en: 'Filling every quarter with deep, far-carrying sound; expert at drawing together the beings of the three worlds for the auspicious assembly; announcing the victory of the king of true *dharma* — the *dundubhi* drum sounds in the sky, proclaiming Your glory.'

meaning_en: 'Verse 45 names the sixth prātihārya — the *dundubhi*, the divine drum that sounds in the sky during the *samavasarana*. The drum&apos;s function in Jain iconography is to announce to all three worlds that a Tirthankara is teaching. This verse and the next three (46, 47, 48) are unique to the Digambara recension; the Shvetambara version concludes at verse 44 (kavi-mudrā). Specific verse placement and exact wording across the Digambara editions vary; reviewer should verify against a canonical Digambara edition (e.g., Bharatiya Jnanpith).'

Verses 45–48 are the four verses unique to the Digambara recension. The Shvetambara stotra concludes at verse 44. These four typically elaborate the prātihāryas — the dundubhi (divine drum), continued chamara/singhasana imagery, divya-dhvani (divine voice), or final benediction — though specific verse placement and wording varies across Digambara editions.
