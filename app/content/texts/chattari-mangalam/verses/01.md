translation_en: |
  Four things are auspicious — the Arihants are auspicious, the Siddhas are auspicious, the sadhus are auspicious, and the Dharma taught by the Kevalis is auspicious.
  Four things are supreme in the universe — the Arihants are supreme, the Siddhas are supreme, the sadhus are supreme, and the Dharma taught by the Kevalis is supreme.
  In four I take refuge — I take refuge in the Arihants, I take refuge in the Siddhas, I take refuge in the sadhus, I take refuge in the Dharma taught by the Kevalis.

meaning_en: |
  The Chattari Mangalam venerates the same four — Arihants, Siddhas, sadhus, and the Kevali-taught Dharma — under three aspects: as auspicious (*mangalam*), as supreme in the universe (*loguttama*), and as objects of refuge (*saranam*).

  Note what *Dharma* means here: not a book, not a rulebook, but the truth as known by those who have attained omniscient knowledge (*kevala-jnana*). The path itself, taught by those who have walked it.

  Note also what is absent: no creator god, no divine intervention, no plea. The structure is the same as the Navkar — recognition of the path and those on it, without solicitation.

What does it mean to take refuge in something that cannot rescue you?

The Chattari Mangalam takes refuge in four. None of them can intervene in your life. The Arihants are still embodied but cannot answer prayers — they have transcended that game. The Siddhas are fully liberated and have no further connection to the world. The sadhus are still walking the path themselves. And the Dharma is not a being at all — it is a path, taught by those who walked it.

This is *refuge* in a specific Jain sense. Not "save me." Not "intervene for me." Closer to: "show me, while I do the walking."

For a generation skeptical of religious promises, this matters. The Chattari Mangalam doesn't promise anything. It identifies what is worth orienting yourself toward — those who attained, those still walking, the path itself — and lets you make of that what you will.

A refuge that does not pretend to save you is the only refuge worth having.
