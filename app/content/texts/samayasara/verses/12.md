bhavarth_hi: 'नौवें अधिकार — *सर्व-विशुद्धि-ज्ञान अधिकार* — से। यह सबसे लम्बा अधिकार है (गाथा २८८-४१५)। इसका विषय है: कैवल्य-ज्ञान — सर्वज्ञता। सर्वज्ञ कैसे हो सकता है? आचार्य कुन्दकुन्द कहते हैं — जो अपनी आत्मा को आत्मा के रूप में जान लेता है, और कर्म से लिप्त नहीं रहता, उसे ही केवल-ज्ञान होता है।\n\nसूक्ष्म बात: सर्वज्ञता कोई बाह्य उपलब्धि नहीं — स्वयं को पूर्णतया जान लेने का परिणाम है। आत्मा का स्वरूप है — सर्व-ज्ञता। जब आवरण हटता है, तो यह स्वरूप प्रकट होता है। केवली स्वयं को जानकर, सब को जानता है।\n\nयह जैन ज्ञान-मीमांसा का गहरा कथन है: ज्ञान बाहर से नहीं आता; भीतर का जो आवरित था, वही प्रकट होता है।'

translation_en: 'The one who knows the soul (as soul) — and is not stained by karmic matter (*pudgala*) — to such a one, *kevala-jnāna* arises, with sight of all substances.'

meaning_en: |
  The ninth chapter (Sarva-viśuddhi-jnāna-adhikāra, gathas 288–415) is the longest in the Samayasara — over 120 gathas on the all-pure omniscient knowing of the kevali. This gatha gives the formal claim.

  Notice the structure of the argument. The kevali does not become omniscient by *acquiring* more knowledge from outside. They become omniscient by *removing* what blocks their natural knowing. The soul, in its true nature, already knows all substances in all their modes; karmic matter is what obscures this. Strip away the karma — by self-knowing and detachment — and what remains is the soul knowing itself. And the soul knowing itself, in its complete nature, *is* knowing everything else, because the soul&apos;s nature includes the capacity for omniscient cognition.

  This is Kundakunda&apos;s most distinctive epistemological claim. Unlike traditions that locate omniscience in a divine being, Jainism locates it in the soul&apos;s own pure nature. Every soul has the capacity. The work is the un-obscuring.

  For a modern reader: this is one of the most ambitious philosophical positions any tradition has ever taken. The claim that any soul, fully purified, would directly know all of reality is breathtaking. It is also internally consistent with the rest of Jain doctrine: no creator, no special grace, no privileged few — every soul is intrinsically what every Tirthankara has manifested.
