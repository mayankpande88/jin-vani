bhavarth_hi: 'समयसार के चौथे अधिकार — *आस्रव अधिकार* — का मूल वर्गीकरण। आस्रव अर्थात् कर्म-पुद्गलों का जीव में प्रवेश। यह क्यों होता है? पाँच कारणों से:\n\n1. **मिथ्यात्व** — सही दृष्टि का अभाव; तत्त्वों को विपरीत मानना।\n2. **अविरति** — व्रत-नियमों से रहित होना; पाप से न रुकना।\n3. **प्रमाद** — असावधानी; धर्म-कार्य में आलस्य।\n4. **कषाय** — क्रोध, मान, माया, लोभ — चार पैसन।\n5. **योग** — मन-वचन-काय की प्रवृत्ति।\n\nइन पाँच के बिना नया कर्म नहीं बँधता। साधक जब इन्हें घटाता है, तो आस्रव रुकता है।'

translation_en: 'The five āsravas are: wrong-belief (mithyātva), non-restraint (avirati), carelessness (pramāda), passions (kaṣāya), and activity of body/speech/mind (yoga). Summarized, the āsravas are five.'

meaning_en: |
  The fourth chapter (Āsrava-adhikāra, gathas 145–164) defines and analyzes the *āsrava* — the influx of karmic matter into the soul. This gatha gives the foundational fivefold classification.

  - **Mithyātva** — wrong belief; treating the categories of reality incorrectly. The most fundamental āsrava because it shapes everything downstream.
  - **Avirati** — non-restraint; the absence of vows / disciplines on harmful action.
  - **Pramāda** — carelessness; spiritual sluggishness, neglect of duties.
  - **Kaṣāya** — the four passions: anger (krodha), pride (māna), deceit (māyā), greed (lobha). Of all the āsravas, these are central — Kundakunda repeatedly identifies kaṣāya as the most consequential.
  - **Yoga** — activity of body, speech, and mind. Even good activity, if mixed with the prior four, brings āsrava.

  This classification is shared with the Tattvartha Sutra (Chapter 6). Without these five, no new karma binds the soul. The Saṁvara chapter (next) tells you how to address each one.
