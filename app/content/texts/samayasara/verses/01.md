translation_en: 'Having bowed to all the Siddhas — those who have attained that firm, unmoving, incomparable state — I shall expound this *Samaya-prābhṛta* (Samayasāra), as taught by the Śruta-Kevalīs.'

meaning_en: |
  The opening *maṅgalācaraṇa* of the Samayasara, with three precise words for the Siddhas: *dhuva* (firm, fixed), *acala* (unmoving), *aṇovama* (incomparable). This is the goal-state — what every soul has the potential to attain.

  The text&apos;s name, *Samaya-prābhṛta*, is itself dense. *Samaya* = the soul (literally, "that which abides in time"). *Prābhṛta* = a treatise or gift. So: "the gift / treatise of the soul." Sometimes translated *Samaya-sāra* — "the essence of the soul."

  Kundakunda&apos;s claim of authority comes through the *Śruta-Kevalīs* — those who fully knew the *śruta* (canonical knowledge) — establishing this teaching as continuous with the tradition&apos;s authoritative lineage.
