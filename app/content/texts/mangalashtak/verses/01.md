bhavarth_hi: 'चार चीज़ें मंगल — शुभ, कल्याणकारी — मानी गयी हैं इस पाठ में: स्वयं भगवान महावीर, उनके प्रथम गणधर गौतम, सबसे प्रभावशाली दिगम्बर आचार्य कुन्दकुन्द, और इन सब के द्वारा संरक्षित जैन धर्म। यह संक्षिप्त पाठ हर शुभ अवसर के आरम्भ में पढ़ा जाता है।'

translation_en: 'Bhagavan Mahavir is auspicious; Gautama Gaṇadhara is auspicious; Acharya Kundakunda is auspicious; let the Jain Dharma be auspicious.'

meaning_en: |
  The Mangala Path is one of the simplest and most-recited Jain prayers in Digambara daily life. It identifies four sources of auspiciousness and acknowledges them by name.

  The four are not chosen randomly. **Mahavir** is the 24th and last Tirthankara — the source of the present teaching tradition. **Gautama** is his Ganadhara — the disciple who organized the teaching into transmissible form. **Kundakunda** is the philosopher whose works most shaped how Digambara Jains understand the soul itself. And **the Jain Dharma** is what they together transmit.

  Notice the structure. The path *acknowledges*; it does not *petition*. There is no "may we be granted," no "save us." The verse simply points to four things and says: *these are auspicious*. Saying so begins one&apos;s day, one&apos;s study, one&apos;s ritual — by orienting the mind toward what is good.
