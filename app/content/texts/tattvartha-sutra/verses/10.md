translation_en: 'These (five kinds of knowledge) are the two *pramāṇas* (i.e., they constitute the two categories of valid knowledge: indirect and direct).'

meaning_en: |
  Just two words. *Tat pramāṇe.* "These (are) the (two) pramāṇas."

  The reference: the five kinds of knowledge from sutra 9 are divided into two groups:

  - **Parokṣa-pramāṇa** (indirect) — *mati* and *śruta*. Knowledge that goes through senses, mind, language. Mediated.
  - **Pratyakṣa-pramāṇa** (direct) — *avadhi*, *manaḥparyaya*, *kevala*. Knowledge that arises directly in the soul, without mediation by senses or words.

  This is a distinctively Jain epistemological move. Other Indian schools tended to treat sense perception as direct (*pratyakṣa*) and inference/testimony as indirect (*parokṣa*). The Jain inversion: sense-based knowledge is actually *indirect* (it goes *through* the senses), and only knowledge that arises *directly* in the soul — clairvoyance, mind-reading, omniscience — is truly direct.

  This shifts the entire frame. The path of liberation, in part, is the soul becoming a direct knower in its own right — not dependent on senses to know.
