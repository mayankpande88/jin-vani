sanskrit: |
  तन्निसर्गादधिगमाद्वा॥३॥
transliteration: |
  tan-nisargād-adhigamād-vā

simple_hi: 'वह सम्यग्दर्शन दो तरीकों से उत्पन्न होता है — अपने स्वभाव से (बिना उपदेश के), या उपदेश से (किसी से सीखकर)।'

simple_en: 'Right faith arises in two ways — naturally (without instruction) or through instruction (learned from another).'

translation_hi: 'वह (सम्यग्दर्शन) निसर्ग से (अपने स्वभाव से) अथवा अधिगम से (उपदेश-शिक्षा से) उत्पन्न होता है।'

bhavarth_hi: 'इस सूत्र में जैन परम्परा का एक उदार दृष्टिकोण व्यक्त है। सम्यग्दर्शन प्राप्त करने के दो मार्ग हैं — कुछ जीवों को बिना किसी बाह्य निमित्त के, अपने आप, सच्ची श्रद्धा हो जाती है। दूसरों को गुरु, शास्त्र, या अनुभव से प्राप्त होती है। दोनों समान रूप से वैध हैं।'

translation_en: 'That (right faith) arises either by nature (*nisarga*, spontaneously, without external instruction) or through instruction (*adhigama*, learning from another).'

meaning_en: |
  The Tattvartha is unusually generous on this point. Right faith is not exclusively the province of those who have studied. Some souls arrive at it spontaneously — by inner clarity, by life experience, by the maturation of the soul through previous births. Others come to it through teaching — a guru, a text, a community.

  Both paths are recognized as legitimate. The sutra does not privilege one over the other. This is theologically generous: the conditions of liberation do not depend on access to scripture, teacher, or institution. They depend on the soul's own readiness, however that readiness arrives.

  *Nisargāj jāyate* — "arises by nature" — means the spontaneous awakening that some souls have. *Adhigamāj jāyate* — "arises through learning" — means the path most of us take. Either gets you there.
