translation_en: 'Mati, śruta, and avadhi can also be *viparyaya* (wrong / inverted).'

meaning_en: |
  An important sutra. Three of the five jnānas — mati, śruta, avadhi — can occur in a *wrong* form when paired with *mithyā-darśana* (wrong belief). Then they are called:

  - **Kumati** — wrong sensory cognition
  - **Kuśruta** — wrong scriptural learning (e.g., heretical texts taken as authoritative)
  - **Vibhaṅga** — wrong avadhi (clairvoyant perception with distorted interpretation)

  Manaḥparyaya and kevala can never be *viparyaya* — they only arise at advanced spiritual stages where mithyā-darśana has been destroyed.

  This sutra is the foundation of the Jain doctrine that *knowledge content* is not enough; *right perception* (samyak-darśana) must underlie it for the knowledge to be liberating. A scholar who has read all the texts but holds them with wrong belief has *kuśruta*, not śruta.
