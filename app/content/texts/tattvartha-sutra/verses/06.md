translation_en: 'The knowledge (*adhigama*) of the categories is achieved through *pramāṇa* (means of valid knowledge in their full scope) and *naya* (perspectival standpoints).'

meaning_en: |
  This sutra introduces *naya-vāda* — the doctrine of perspectival standpoints — which is one of Jainism's distinctive contributions to world philosophy.

  *Pramāṇa* refers to the full means of valid knowledge — direct perception, inference, scriptural testimony in their complete form. *Naya* refers to a single-perspective standpoint that gives a partial but valid view.

  The example: take a clay pot. *Dravyārthika-naya* (substance perspective) sees it as clay — its substance never changes; only the form does. *Paryāyārthika-naya* (modal perspective) sees it as a pot — clay is now in pot-form, having previously been lump-form. Both views are correct; neither is complete.

  This is the seed of *anekāntavāda* — the doctrine of multiple viewpoints — that develops more fully later in Jain philosophy. Built into the foundational text from the start: epistemic humility. Knowledge advances through *both* total view and perspectival view, and acknowledges the limits of any single perspective.
