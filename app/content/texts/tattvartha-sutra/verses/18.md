transliteration: |
  vyañjanasya-avagrahaḥ

simple_hi: 'व्यञ्जन (परोक्ष-सूचक) का (केवल) अवग्रह होता है।'

simple_en: 'For *vyañjana* (the indistinct, non-articulated phase of sense-stimulus), (only) avagraha occurs.'

translation_hi: 'व्यञ्जन (अव्यक्त-सूक्ष्म पदार्थ) का (केवल) अवग्रह होता है।'

bhavarth_hi: 'जैन तर्क-शास्त्र में दो प्रकार के अवग्रह हैं — व्यञ्जन-अवग्रह और अर्थ-अवग्रह। *व्यञ्जन* अर्थात् बहुत प्रारम्भिक, अव्यक्त संवेदना — अभी पदार्थ का स्पष्ट बोध नहीं हुआ। *अर्थ* अर्थात् पदार्थ का बोध। पहले व्यञ्जन-अवग्रह होता है (केवल अवग्रह की अवस्था में रुक जाता है — आगे के तीन चरण नहीं होते), फिर अर्थ-अवग्रह।'

translation_en: 'Of *vyañjana* (the implicit, indistinct stimulus), (only) avagraha occurs (i.e., the other three stages do not apply at this prior level).'

meaning_en: |
  A subtle distinction. *Vyañjana* refers to the very earliest, indistinct stage of sensory contact — when something is registered but not yet articulated as an object. The Jain analysis: at this proto-cognitive level, only avagraha (initial apprehension) operates; the further stages of īhā, avāya, dhāraṇā do not yet apply.

  Once the cognition rises to *artha* (an articulated object — sutra 1.17), all four stages become relevant.

  This is precise philosophy of perception: there is a cognitive moment before there is a "thing" cognized. That pre-thing moment has its own structure.
