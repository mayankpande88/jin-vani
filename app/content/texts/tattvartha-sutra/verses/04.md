translation_en: 'The seven categories are: jīva (soul), ajīva (non-soul), āsrava (influx of karma), bandha (bondage), saṁvara (stoppage), nirjarā (shedding), and mokṣa (liberation).'

meaning_en: |
  The most important sutra of Chapter 1 after the opening. The seven categories cover the entire structure of existence in Jain philosophy.

  - **Jīva** — soul; the conscious principle, infinite in number
  - **Ajīva** — non-soul; everything not conscious (matter, space, time, motion, rest)
  - **Āsrava** — influx; the inflow of karmic matter into the soul
  - **Bandha** — bondage; the binding of karma to soul
  - **Saṁvara** — stoppage; halting the inflow of new karma
  - **Nirjarā** — shedding; the gradual removal of accumulated karma
  - **Mokṣa** — liberation; complete freedom from karma

  Notice the structure. The categories are not random — they describe a process. *Jīva* and *ajīva* are the fundamental divide; *āsrava* and *bandha* describe how the soul gets entangled; *saṁvara* and *nirjarā* describe the way out; *mokṣa* is the goal. This is the architecture of the Jain path.

  Some Shvetambara sources list nine (adding *puṇya* — merit, and *pāpa* — demerit). The Digambara tradition typically holds to seven, with merit and demerit subsumed under *bandha*.
