The 24 Ford-Makers

The 24 Tirthankaras

The 24 Tirthankaras of the present avasarpiṇī time-cycle, in canonical order. Each shown with their lāñchana (identifying symbol), traditional complexion, parents, birthplace, and place of mokṣa.

Tirthankaras are not gods (see Foundation 01). They are liberated souls who, in their final birth, established the four-fold saṅgha and showed the path of mokṣa.

#24Golden

Mahavira (Vardhamana)

महावीर (वर्धमान)

Lāñchana
Lion (siṁha)(सिंह)
Father
राजा सिद्धार्थ
Mother
महारानी त्रिशला (in Shvetambara: also called Priyakarini)
Birthplace
Kundalapura (Vaishali region)
Mokṣa
Pavapuri

The 24th and last Tirthankara of this time-cycle (599–527 BCE in traditional dating). NOT the founder of Jainism — the last in a long line. His teaching emphasized the five great vows and forms the basis of post-canonical Jain practice.

Note on this tableNames, lāñchanas, complexions, and parentage follow the Digambara tradition. The Glossary entry on Tirthankara covers the doctrinal definition; Foundation 01 covers the misconception that Tirthankaras are gods. Most Tirthankaras attain mokṣa at Sammed Shikhar (in present-day Jharkhand), the most sacred Jain pilgrimage site. All entries draft pending Digambara cultural reviewer.
