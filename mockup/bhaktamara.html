Bhaktamara Stotra

📜 48 verses (Digambara) ⏱ ~15 min reading 🎵 Audio · Phase 1.5 📖 Read · Study · Listen

The Bhaktamara Stotra is one of the most beloved devotional hymns in Jainism, composed in praise of Bhagavan Adinath — Rishabhanatha, the first Tirthankara — by Acharya Manatunga. Its 48 verses are recited daily across the Digambara tradition.

Tradition holds that Acharya Manatunga composed the stotra while bound in 48 chains by King Bhoja of Ujjayini, who had heard Manatunga's yogic powers. With each verse, one chain miraculously broke; by the 48th, he was free. The four additional verses unique to this Digambara recension are traditionally associated with the breaking of the final four chains.

The stotra is rich in poetic imagery — the radiance of the Tirthankara's feet illuminates the jewels in the crowns of bowing celestials; the ocean of worldly existence finds its only refuge in the Jina. Reading it as a daily practice cultivates *bhakti* — devotion grounded in understanding rather than mere ritual.
