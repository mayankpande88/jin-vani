All sites

  • Siddhakshetra

    Sammed Shikharji

    Jharkhand · Giridih

    The most important Jain pilgrimage. Twenty of the twenty-four Tirthankaras attained moksha here on the Parasnath hills.

  • Siddhakshetra

    Pavapuri

    Bihar · Nalanda

    Where Mahavira attained moksha in 527 BCE. The Jal Mandir — a temple in the middle of a lotus pond — marks the site.

  • Siddhakshetra

    Girnar

    Gujarat · Junagadh

    Where Tirthankara Neminatha attained moksha. One of the seven principal Jain pilgrimage destinations.

  • Siddhakshetra

    Champapuri

    Bihar · Bhagalpur

    Where Tirthankara Vasupujya attained moksha — and the only one of his five kalyanakas not at Sammed Shikharji.

  • Heritage / TempleDigambara

    Shravanabelagola

    Karnataka · Hassan

    57-foot monolithic Bahubali (Gomateshvara) statue, carved 981 CE. The Mahamastakabhisheka head-anointing is held every 12 years.

  • Atishaya KshetraDigambara

    Mangi-Tungi

    Maharashtra · Nashik

    Twin sacred peaks with rock-carved Tirthankara idols. Home to the 108-foot Rishabhanatha statue (2016) — world's tallest Jain idol.
