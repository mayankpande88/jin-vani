Authors · c. 7th–8th century CE

Acharya Manatunga

आचार्य मानतुङ्ग

Composer of the Bhaktamara Stotra — one of the most beloved devotional hymns in all of Jainism.

Almost nothing is preserved of Manatunga's historical life. What survives is the Bhaktamara Stotra: 48 verses (Digambara recension; 44 in Shvetambara) of remarkable Sanskrit poetry, dense with imagery, technically masterful, devotionally profound. The famous legend — that Manatunga composed the stotra while bound in 48 chains by King Bhoja, with each verse breaking one chain — comes from later sources and varies across traditions.

Significance

For over 1300 years, the Bhaktamara has been recited daily across the Digambara world — in temples, in homes, by elders, by children just learning their first prayers. It is the living heart of Jain devotional practice.
