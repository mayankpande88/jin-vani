The Acharyas

The seers, scholars, and householders whose works form this library — what we know about them, what they wrote, and why their writing still matters today.

Acharya Kundakunda

आचार्य कुन्दकुन्द

c. 2nd century CE (sometimes earlier)

The towering figure of Digambara Jain philosophy. His Samayasara is the most influential text in the tradition.

Acharya Umaswami

आचार्य उमास्वामी

c. 2nd–4th century CE

Author of the Tattvartha Sutra — the rare Jain scripture accepted as authoritative by both Digambara and Shvetambara traditions.

Read more →

Acharya Pujyapada

आचार्य पूज्यपाद

c. 5th–6th century CE

Author of the Sarvarthasiddhi — the foundational Digambara commentary on Tattvartha — and the Ishtopadesh.

Read more →

Acharya Samantabhadra

आचार्य समन्तभद्र

c. 2nd century CE (some place earlier)

Composer of the Aapta-mīmāṁsā (philosophical defense of the trustworthy guide) and the Ratnakaranda Shravakachara (lay ethics).

Read more →

Acharya Manatunga

आचार्य मानतुङ्ग

c. 7th–8th century CE

Composer of the Bhaktamara Stotra — one of the most beloved devotional hymns in all of Jainism.

Pandit Daulatram Kasliwal

पण्डित दौलतराम कासलीवाल

18th century

Author of Chhah Dhala — the comprehensive Hindi summary that most modern Digambaras read daily. Foundational figure of the Digambara Terapanthi tradition.
